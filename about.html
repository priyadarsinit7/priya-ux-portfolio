Designer, researcher,
former educator.

I came to UX design through an unusual path — I spent years as a preschool and computer science teacher before pivoting to design. Those roles gave me something most designers have to learn over time: how to truly observe people, adapt to their individual needs, and communicate complexity in clear, accessible ways.

My MCA background in software development rounds out my analytical thinking — I understand how products are built, which helps me design within real constraints and collaborate more meaningfully with engineers.

"I believe great design starts with listening — to users, to data, and to the spaces in between where frustration quietly lives."

For my capstone project, I worked on Bollystep — a platform to help users like Sanjana, who wanted to organize a dance performance for her wedding. She struggled with coordinating choreography across different schedules, time zones, and experience levels. I designed a tool that helps users learn dances asynchronously while streamlining logistics to reduce stress.

I'm excited to apply my skills to projects that bridge the gap between user needs and business goals. I believe in an empathetic, collaborative, and iterative approach to design — and I'm just getting started.

How I Work

Design principles
I actually believe in.

👂

Listen first, design second

Every design decision should trace back to something a real user said, did, or felt. I start with research — not assumptions.

🔄

Iterate, don't over-polish

The best design is never done. I test early, gather feedback, and improve — rather than waiting for perfection that never arrives.

🤝

Collaborate across silos

Great products come from designers, engineers, and stakeholders working together — not in isolation. I make that collaboration easier.

🎯

Simplicity is the goal

Reducing friction, clarifying hierarchy, and removing cognitive load are design acts of respect — for users and for their time.

The Problem

Meaningful products, lost in a confusing experience

Mayura Roots is an e-commerce platform selling Indian traditional and cultural products. While the products themselves are unique and meaningful, the website experience made it difficult for users to discover and understand them.

Three compounding problems: The navigation is crowded and unclear, product information lacks cultural context, and the overall design reduces trust and clarity for shoppers — particularly first-time buyers who have no prior familiarity with the products.
Goal

Navigate the culture, not just the catalogue

Redesign the website's primary navigation and information architecture to make the shopping experience clearer, more engaging, and culturally informative — so users can discover products while learning about their significance.

Research Methods

Six methods to understand a complex problem

Used the Double Diamond methodology to structure the research and design process, ensuring insights drove every decision.

Heuristic Evaluation

Evaluated the existing website against established usability heuristics. Found multiple violations around navigation consistency, unclear affordances, error prevention, and lack of flexibility for different user types.

Content Audit

Reviewed all existing content for clarity, structure, and accuracy. Found that product descriptions were sparse, cultural context was almost entirely absent, and the content hierarchy on key pages was confusing.

Competitive & Comparative Analysis

Analyzed competing and comparable cultural e-commerce sites to understand best practices in navigation design, product storytelling, and trust-building. Identified patterns that work and gaps in the market.

Card Sorting

Conducted card sorting with participants to understand their mental models for organizing cultural products. Results directly informed the new navigation categories and site hierarchy.

User Interviews

Spoke with a range of potential users — from those familiar with Indian cultural products to complete newcomers. Revealed that users want to understand the meaning, origin, and usage of products before purchasing, not just see price and photos.

Information Architecture Analysis

Mapped the existing site structure to identify gaps, orphaned pages, and illogical groupings. Provided a baseline for the redesigned sitemap.

Key Insights

Four themes that shaped the redesign

🗺️

Navigation is the core issue

Users often felt lost — too many options, no clear categorization, and no sense of where they were or where to go next.

📖

Lack of cultural storytelling

Users wanted to understand the meaning, origin, and usage of products before purchasing — not just see a product name and price.

🛡️

Trust & credibility issues

Outdated design and limited product information reduced confidence in the authenticity and quality of products.

🧭

Users want to be guided

First-time buyers expected the website to guide them through cultural products — like a knowledgeable shopkeeper would in a physical store.

User Archetypes

Two distinct types of shoppers

Primary

Purposeful Product Seekers

Users who visit the site with a clear intent to purchase a specific cultural product — but rely on the website to understand its meaning and usage before buying.

Secondary

Marketplace Meanderers

Casual browsers who explore for inspiration and cultural discovery rather than immediate purchase. They want to learn and be surprised.

Problem Statement

Purposeful product seekers need a clearer way to explore and purchase

Purposeful product seekers need a clear and trustworthy way to explore and purchase cultural products because the current website lacks intuitive navigation, detailed product information, and cultural storytelling.

How Might We…

  • Help users understand the cultural significance of products before purchasing?
  • Simplify navigation for easier product discovery?
  • Modernize the visual design while preserving the cultural identity of the brand?
  • Guide users through the shopping experience as if they were browsing in a physical cultural store?
Design Strategy

A shopping experience that feels like cultural exploration

The core design strategy was to create a shopping experience that feels like exploring a cultural marketplace — not just browsing a product catalogue. Four areas of focus:

Focus AreaDesign Direction
Navigation claritySimplified menu structure based on card sorting results — fewer, clearer categories
Information architectureNew sitemap with logical groupings, better hierarchy, and improved product discoverability
Product storytellingEnhanced product pages with cultural context, origin information, and usage guidance
Trust & credibilityModern visual refresh while preserving cultural identity — improved photography standards, cleaner layouts
Information Architecture

Restructuring the foundation of the experience

The primary design change was simplifying the navigation structure and organizing products in an intuitive way. A new sitemap was created to support easier product discovery and better reflect how users actually think about cultural products.

  • Clearer product categories based on card sorting results
  • Better grouping of related cultural products by occasion, tradition, and type
  • Simplified navigation menu — fewer top-level items, more logical sub-categories
  • Improved overall site hierarchy — users always know where they are

Usability Testing

Validating navigation and cultural clarity

Usability testing was conducted on the redesigned prototype to evaluate whether the new navigation and site structure actually solved the problems identified in research.

Testing validated three things:

  • Whether users could find products more easily with the new navigation
  • Whether users could understand product meaning and cultural significance
  • Whether users could navigate the website without confusion or dead ends

Key results:

  • The redesigned navigation significantly reduced browsing friction — users located products faster with fewer wrong turns
  • Users reported feeling more confident and engaged when browsing the redesigned interface
  • Cultural context on product pages was described as "genuinely helpful" — users felt better equipped to make purchasing decisions
Outcome

A marketplace that honours what it sells

The redesigned website creates a more structured and engaging shopping experience by combining clear navigation with cultural storytelling. The new design allows users to discover products more easily while learning about the traditions and cultural heritage behind them.
